Most car wash owners check one number: total revenue. But that single metric hides more than it reveals.

## The Metrics That Matter

### Revenue Per Work Post

Not all posts are equal. If Post 1 generates 40% of your revenue while Post 4 sits idle, you have a utilization problem — not a demand problem.

**Action:** Rebalance service assignments. Move popular services to underused posts.

### Average Order Value

Are customers buying the basic wash or the premium package? If your average is dropping, your upsell strategy needs work.

**Action:** Train staff to recommend add-ons. Position premium services first in your online booking page.

### Cancellation Rate by Source

Online bookings might have a 5% cancel rate while phone bookings hit 20%. The channel matters.

**Action:** Push customers toward lower-cancel-rate channels. Add confirmation steps where needed.

### Peak Hours vs. Staffing

Your busiest hours should have your best staff. Seems obvious — but without data, most owners guess wrong.

**Action:** Overlay order volume with scheduled staff. Gaps = lost revenue. Overlap = wasted payroll.

### Client Retention Rate

How many first-time visitors come back within 30 days? If the number is below 30%, your service quality or follow-up needs attention.

**Action:** Track return visits per client. Set up a "we miss you" reminder for clients who haven't visited in 30+ days.

## From Data to Decisions

Raw numbers are useless without action. The best car wash operators review their analytics weekly and make one small adjustment each time.

Over a year, 52 small improvements compound into a fundamentally different business.
